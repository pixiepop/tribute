Ada Lovelace

The first woman in tech

A little about Ada's life
  • Ada Lovelace is known for her mathematical works in collaboration with the "Father of the Computer", Charles Babbage.
  • Ada was initiated into the profession by her mother, who feared that poetry would spoil the young woman's morals.
  • Ada also harboured a love for poetry, and preferred to blend the seemingly unrelated subjects, in her work.
  • Ada liked calling herself an "Analyst (& Metaphysician)", working on what could be termed "poetical science".
  • Her initial works with Babbage, earned her fame and recognition.
  • Ada's private life was full of confusion and heartbreak, she made up for by her astounding scientific work.
  • Despite a short life, Lovelace left her mark in the history of mathematics and computers.
